Sinta Saúde: Designing a mobile app for a local doctor's office

In an ever-evolving digital world, a health clinic's online presence is vital for connecting with patients in an effective and informative way.

This project came about when a friend of mine, who works at this clinic, asked me to help her develop a visual example of what their mobile app could look like. They wanted to expand their presence to mobile devices, creating an experience that would bring the richness of their website directly into the palm of their users hands.

I was given the various pieces of content that should be in the app and the rest was up to me. However, this project didn't take off, as they gave up on the idea, but I still took the opportunity to develop my skills and took it as far as I could.

Project Overview

Challenge

The challenge was clear: to faithfully reproduce the content and aesthetics of the existing Sinta Saúde website in an accessible and aesthetically appealing mobile application. Also integrate some extra content that was shared with me.

Solution

Staying true to the existing design and content, I worked to adapt every element for a perfect mobile experience. Every page and section was carefully transposed to the mobile version, ensuring that users had access to the clinic's essential information quickly and intuitively.

Research

Content Analysis

As already mentioned, this project is mainly about reproducing the web page in a mobile app, as such, an in-depth analysis of the competition was not carried out. Instead, I focused on analyzing the existing website content structure, types of information, and multimedia elements in order to understand the organization and how information is presented to users. This will help to determine which content elements are essential for mobile users and prioritize them.

To ensure the design aligns with the established brand identity and to maintain visual consistency between the website and the mobile app, we're gonna use the same guidelines, including colors, typography, imagery and speach.

At least but not least, I analyzed the navigation menu to figure out how it could be transformed into an intuitive, easy-to-use mobile menu.

Insights

Based on research into user preferences and industry best practices, this app has been crafted to align with the needs and expectations of healthcare consumers. Here are key insights that have informed the design of this app:

  • Information Prioritization: Certain information sections, such as contact details, services, and appointment booking, are highly prioritized by users, indicating their essential nature for the mobile app;
  • Quick Access: Users prefer quick access to vital information without extensive scrolling, suggesting a need for concise and organized content presentation;
  • Content Condensation: The website contains extensive textual content that needs to be condensed for mobile screens, requiring careful selection of key messages and concise wording;
  • Visual Elements: High-quality images and multimedia elements enhance user engagement;
  • Intuitive Navigation: Users expect intuitive navigation patterns similar to standard mobile apps, emphasizing the need for a clear and straightforward menu structure;
  • Consistent Branding: Consistency in branding elements, such as color schemes and typography, is essential for brand recognition and user trust;
  • Innovative Features: Exploration of innovative features in competitor apps, such as integrated chat support or appointment scheduling, can inspire unique functionalities for the mobile app.

Define & Ideate

Prioritize Features

Quality prevailed over quantity as we chose to refine essential functionalities, ensuring each feature meets the highest standards of our users. Two features were immediately crossed off the list as they were not the clinic's priority and given that they still offer these services through third-party apps.

Features that stay in the game:

  • Specialties: the aim is to show the user all the specialties available and provide detailed information about each one. When clicking the button to make an appointment, the user is redirected to WhatsApp, where he can communicate in real time with the clinic and schedule an appointment;
  • Explore: here the user can explore some pathologies, their causes and treatments;
  • Staff board: introducing a staff board ensures transparent communication, fosters trust, and enhances patient-staff interactions;
  • Highlights: activities provided by the clinic, such as workshops, will appear prominently on the home page;
  • FAQ: so that there are no doubts left, the most frequently asked questions will be available for any user to have access to.

Features that were excluded:

  • Appointment scheduling: seamless appointment scheduling empowers users with convenience, saves time, and enhances their overall experience;
  • Online consultation: offering online consultations expands accessibility, providing timely healthcare services from the comfort of one's home, ensuring convenience and continuity of care for users.

Branding

UI Kit

Using the same colors and typography as the website, including the distinct tones of the logo and incorporating the main color already featured in some elements, was a purposeful choice deeply rooted in improving brand recognition and user experience.
By seamlessly integrating these elements, we ensure visual continuity that resonates with users transition from the website to our app. The logo's familiar colors create an immediate connection, reinforcing the clinic's identity, while the consistent use of the main color on main buttons establishes a visual hierarchy and intuitively guides user interactions.

Test

Test Responses

Using the high fidelity, limited functionality prototype I created, I started to test my design on users to measure its usability and identify any areas of improvement. Since the app was not fully developed and the project became personal, only three participants were recruited.
All of them performed the same three tasks to allow comparison and all participants managed to complete them successfully.
Once the tests were finished, I gave them the opportunity to freely explore the interface and a brief exit questionnaire was carried out where participants were able to give their evaluation and suggestions. The prototype obtained an average rating of 7.5 out of 10 mainly because it wasn't fully developed.

Priority Revisions

To my surprise there was only one thing that was pointed out by all participants:

  • The readability of the text inserted above the image in the for you section on the home page.

Reflection & Next Steps

The resulting design not only aligns with the clinic's brand identity but also promises a seamless user experience, offering intuitive interactions and empowering patients in their healthcare journey. If I were to push this project further, I would:

1. Re-test

To validate the revisions made, I would conduct another round of testing with users.

2. Design Handoff

I would then present the final design to the technical director of the clinic and hand it off to developers to build the app.

3. Future Implementations

I think there’s room to add more value and delight to the experience by implementing the eliminated features previously referenced on "Prioritize Features".
