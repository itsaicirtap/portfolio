Context

Have you ever struggled to use a website or app because of a disability? Inclue, an interactive web documentary concept, aims to bridge this gap. Developed by our team of four for our Master's program in New Media and Web Practices, Inclue uses real stories and simulations to explore the challenges of navigating the digital world with accessibility barriers.

We created a primary video that initially presents all disabilities simultaneously to all users. After watching it, users can select a ‚Äúcharacter‚Äù and experience the video from the perspective of their specific disability. This approach helps users understand the challenges faced by each "character" and is complemented by excerpts from interviews and images provided by them. On the project page, we also present the ideal version of the video, accessible to everyone.

This project is essentially an interactive web documentary focused on accessibility and digital inclusion. Users can choose paths that reflect various perspectives based on different disabilities (visual, hearing, etc.), adopting an exploratory approach by navigating through predefined options that connect to multimedia content.

Unfortunately, due to time constraints, we were unable to complete this project. However, I believe the concept is relevant and worth sharing. What follows is a sketch of our ideas.

Research

Behind the Screens

We decided to conduct interviews with two types of users: computer engineers or programmers (ranging from students to senior developers) and professionals in the field of digital content creation. These two profiles would help us better understand the digital product market and why accessibility is still lacking in both interfaces and content.

We created a brief script to ensure consistency across all interviews. Each member conducted two interviews, which took place either in person or remotely.

Accessibility Overlooked üöß

Interface developers often do not prioritize accessibility in digital platforms, which can prevent people with disabilities from accessing content due to the absence of necessary resources and accommodations.

Our Proposal üí°

An interactive platform designed to simulate various disabilities can allow typical users to experience content as if they had those disabilities, providing an immersive understanding of accessibility challenges.

Connecting the Dots

After transcribing the interviews, we analyzed and grouped the responses. Each team member selected relevant excerpts, placing them on sticky notes in Miro. We then reviewed the similarities, which we grouped together to form our affinity map.

Gathering Findings

Main findings identified:

There is a tenuous understanding of what a screen reader is, with the majority having never seen one in use.

While awareness has improved, there's still a significant lack of understanding on the subject.

It seems that companies tend to prioritize content for the common user, and accessibility is not considered a priority.

While participants are aware of various disabilities, none of them are familiar with the daily challenges faced by individuals with disabilities.

The Big Picture

Our primary objective is to shed light on the challenges faced by individuals with disabilities in the digital realm.

We aim to create an immersive scrolling experience that highlights the challenges faced by people with disabilities. By subtly revealing content, we hope to shed light on potential solutions directly from those affected.

Users will be presented with a compelling choice. Split into three, the screen offers tantalizing photo glimpses of different experiences. These visual cues are designed to pique curiosity and encourage users to delve deeper into the stories behind the images.

Once a user selects an experience, a tailored video begins, showcasing the specific disability. To simulate color blindness, image colors and contrast will be adjusted. Deafness will be represented through a silent image. Dyslexia will be portrayed through altered text, such as letter substitutions or dense text blocks, hindering readability. After this video concludes, a simple scroll reveals the full testimony and additional details.

We determined that a persistent navigation bar would hinder readability and detract from the overall aesthetic. Instead, users can navigate using footer links or the dedicated 'Back' and 'Next Experience' buttons located at the end of each page.

To provide comprehensive information, we decided to include an "About Us" page highlighting the team, an "About the Project" page detailing our concept and goals, and a "Know More" section offering contact details for relevant associations.

Branding

Inclue

Inclue emerged from the need for a concise name encapsulating both the documentary's form and content. Combining "inclusive" and "clue," the name symbolizes the project's focus on understanding diverse experiences and offering valuable insights.

After exploring various design concepts in Adobe Illustrator, we selected a geometric interpretation of the word "Inclue" for our logo. This approach aimed for a visually striking yet accessible representation of the project. User testing confirmed the logo's readability and overall effectiveness. To enhance recognition and readability, subtle adjustments were made to specific letterforms without compromising the geometric aesthetic.

Built on the Roboto font, the logo utilizes diverse geometric shapes to symbolize inclusion and unity. This approach creates a versatile visual identity that can be expanded upon for future design elements. A color contrast test using the Dalton Chrome plugin verified the logo's accessibility for individuals with color blindness.

Designing for Consistency

To engage a younger audience, we selected a vibrant color palette featuring calming blue, energizing yellow, and wise purple. Prioritizing accessibility, we ensured high-contrast colors suitable for color blindness, including the less common Tritanopia. The sans-serif Roboto font, chosen for its readability, will be prominent throughout the website to accommodate users with visual impairments or dyslexia.

Testing the Waters

To gather insights into user experiences, we conducted usability tests with four participants who interacted with an early-stage prototype of our website. Given the project's exploratory nature, we adopted an open-ended testing approach, allowing users to navigate freely and provide feedback. This enabled us to assess navigation, information discovery, and overall comprehension of the project's goals.

Is there anything we should improve?
Hide the navigation bar on the first page to emphasize the interactive documentary format.
Add more arrows to guide users after video playback.
Preserve the documentary's visual integrity by removing distracting YouTube elements and creating unique thumbnails for each video.
Include explicit prompts encouraging users to rewatch seemingly identical clips to fully grasp the intended experience.

Improve text clarity, cohesion, and readability by adjusting font size.
Offer written accessibility suggestions for improving digital accessibility.

Final Design

An Experience for Everyone!

Shared Challenges

The homepage features a minimalist intro screen with the logo and essential user guidance. A brief introductory video follows, setting the project's "What would it be like if..." theme.

Choose Your Path

A split screen presents three experience choices after the intro. Each option includes a conceptual image and brief interviewee details. Selecting an option initiates a tailored video simulating a specific disability.

Voices from the Digital Frontier

Intimate testimonials from individuals facing digital barriers follow the simulated experience. Through personal stories and accompanying visuals, the project aims to foster empathy and inspire innovative solutions for inclusive design.

Conclusion

Reflection & Next Steps

Exploring inclusion and accessibility through this project provided valuable insights from those directly affected. While the project's outcomes are promising, its full potential remains untapped due to the unfinished interactive elements. Future development should prioritize enhancing these elements, alongside implementing user feedback. The project's emphasis on interactive storytelling and user-centered design has the potential to significantly impact awareness and understanding of digital accessibility.
