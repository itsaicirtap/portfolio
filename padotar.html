Padotar: Helping people find the perfect pet or related educational and informative content

This project is part of my master's thesis, so I will only refer to the content that actually corresponds to the mobile app and its research. However, if you are interested in reading the full text, you can do so here.

In this project, I aimed to combine my love for animals with addressing a serious public health problem. Currently, there is a significant increase in the number of abandoned animals, leading to a worrying situation that places a burden on animal protection and shelter associations. Many animal shelters have social media accounts, where most of their advertising takes place. However, unlike these platforms where it's not possible to separate posts by topic, this app offers a variety of content categorized to provide an intuitive user experience. The primary goal is to facilitate the adoption process, enhance communication with shelters, and disseminate not only their activities but also educational content frequently sought by pet owners.

Padotar is a fictitious shelter created purely for the project. The conception of the mobile app was restricted to the process of developing a digital product from a design perspective and was not implemented after the evaluation of the interactive hi-fi prototype.

Project Overview

Problem

No animal shelter in the research area offers a mobile app with adoption features, direct communication and properly organized informative and educational content.

Solution

Developing a mobile app that can be replicated by any animal shelter. The app incorporates features such as adoption functionalities, direct communication, and well-organized informative and educational content, all designed to potentially enhance adoption rates.

Research

Local Shelters Analysis

An analysis of the panorama of local animal shelters and the strategies implemented online revealed the following:

  • 17 have a Facebook profile;
  • 13 have their own website;
  • 11 have an Instagram profile;
  • 2 of them have a Youtube channel.

However, none of the 18 shelters have their own mobile app.

Competitive Analysis

To gain insights into the strengths and weaknesses of potential competitors, I conducted a brief survey of similar apps existing in Portugal. Despite the limited scope of my search, I identified only one website that matched the search criteria, and it's not even a mobile app.
The platform in question is Petify, an interactive platform with the primary goal of assisting abandoned animals in finding a family. Petify operates essentially like a "lost and found," allowing individuals or shelters to post advertisements for adoptions or lost animals.

Strengths

  • Increased visibility;
  • Community engagement;
  • Promotes civic responsibility;
  • Quick dissemination of information;
  • Educational opportunities.

Weaknesses

  • Accuracy of information;
  • Overwhelming volume;
  • Authenticity and privacy concerns;
  • Confirmation of adoptions;
  • Managing found pets.

Interviews

To conduct these interviews, individuals working in institutions dedicated to the animal cause were selected to understand the needs of shelters, treating the shelters as our stakeholders.
Two interviews were conducted, during which participants shared problems and needs, providing valuable information for the project's development. In total, twelve questions were asked, covering the general context of the shelter, the adoption process, the impact of new media, and the implementation of alternative digital tools.
Subsequently, in a brief informal conversation with two potential users on the research topic, relevant information emerged. This information not only aided in defining the functionalities to be implemented but also in creating personas that reflect real experiences.

Findings

Summary of findings identified through literature review and interviews:

  • Size, behavior and personality are determining factors in adoption;
  • Give visibility to sponsorships, donations, members and volunteering;
  • It is important to raise awareness among future adopters;
  • Provide news about the shelter and the activities carried out by them;
  • Solidarity sales contribute to the financial sustainability of the shelter;
  • Maintain a coherent aesthetic and positive speech;
  • Clarify common doubts among pet owners.

Personas

These fictional characters represent the ideal users for the digital platform. In this case, they are based not only on the findings gathered from the interviews but also on the experiences and needs of real people I briefly spoke with about the subject of this project. Three personas were created to match the three different types of users identified: people who work in shelters, people who have already adopted an animal, and people who intend to adopt.

Define & Ideate

Prioritize Features

Due to the inherent temporal limitations within the project scope, I was confronted with the challenge of carefully selecting and prioritizing features for the app's design. The extensive list of proposed features needed to be streamlined in the face of limited time resources, so I decided to remove two features that were probably going to take more time to create and yet didn't affect the main purpose of the project.

Features that move on to the next stage:

  • Search Filter: Allows the user to select the desired characteristics when searching for a pet;
  • Adopt: Displays all available pets with detailed information and an adoption form that is sent directly to the shelter;
  • Quiz: An initial optional multiple-choice questionnaire, assessing readiness to adopt;
  • News: Provides updates on the shelter's work and activities carried out;
  • Articles: Offers educational/ informative content to guide decisions and responsibilities of pet adoption;
  • Training: Includes step-by-step training content for pet owners to replicate at home;
  • How to Help: Highlights information on sponsorships, partners, volunteers, and donation methods;
  • FAQ: Addresses frequently asked questions to clarify the adoption process.

Features that were excluded:

  • Pet Hotel Map: Incorporating an interactive map of pet hotels, designed to enhance the pet owners' experience by providing detailed, real-time information on available accommodations for their pets' stay;
  • Solidary Shop: Since this is a common resource among shelters, it would have been interesting to integrate a small solidarity store. In this store, all sales would have gone toward the financial sustainability of the association.

Branding

Branding

Now that i have refined the overall structure of the app, the question now was - what will it look like? Before working on the UI, I focused on creating Padotar's branding to effectively communicate the brand personality through the app.

Logo Design

Given the importance of visual identity in the implementation of digital products, I considered it pertinent to create a graphic brand. I tried, above all, to maintain a simple and minimalist aesthetic to provide a legible and visually impactful image.
Therefore, the first step was to select the name of the fictitious shelter for which the application was developed. Since it is a shelter for animals looking for a family and the comfort of a home, I combined the words 'para' and 'adotar'. These form the word 'padotar,' which, coincidentally, results in a sound effect similar to their hurried pronunciation.
After selecting the name, I began experimenting with the graphic brand, using the digital tool Adobe Illustrator. As seen in the figure below, a brief study of colors was carried out, always associated with neutral tones that refer to pet fur. Above all, my goal was to maintain a simple and minimalist aesthetic, ensuring a legible and visually impactful image.

Once the brand was defined, I introduced the typography, giving rise to the logo.

Padotar's brand image is defined by a symbol depicting a dog and a cat inside a shelter, representing the most common animals found in these institutions. The logo, unmistakably corresponding to the shelter's name, complements this symbol.
The main color, orange, was chosen for its contrast with other elements, conveying the shelter's values of joy, trust, strength, and ambition. To ensure consistency with the interface design, this color will be integrated into various visual components.
The logo features a simple sans-serif font, Futura, in a bold style, chosen to visually balance and emphasize the shelter's name.

Brand Guide

I focused on the essentials here, as it wasn't the main purpose of the project.

UI Kit

In order to enhance the user experience, I opted for a vibrant and personality-filled color - orange. Complementing this, I chose a pastel shade that represents an analogous color to orange, aiming to evoke serenity and comfort. Black is applied to the text with RGB values below 100% to avoid creating excessive contrast, which could strain the vision of users spending extended periods in the app.
In selecting fonts, my goal was simplicity, with a focus on enhancing readability for individuals with reduced vision, dyslexia, or cognitive and neurological difficulties. To maintain visual coherence, I opted for two sans-serif fonts: Futura, which was previously used in the logo and is applied to the titles, and Avenir Next, which is used for all other written content. This distinction aids in differentiating headings from paragraphs of text.

Prototype

Lo-fi Wireframe Sketches

This is when I began making decisions on how to organize the content on Padotar's app, aligning with the project goals I aimed to achieve. Subsequently, I developed a low-fidelity wireframe of the main pages, using basic geometric shapes and minimal details to emphasize the flow and features.

Paper Prototype

At this stage, it becomes crucial to delve into the interface to visualize the complete user flow and explore ways to make the process of searching for and adopting a pet as intuitive as possible. This allows us to gain an initial understanding of how the interface will behave when users engage in more specific tasks.

Two individuals were invited to analyze and provide feedback on the prototype to assess the intuitiveness of the flows. Consequently, I opted to simplify and streamline some of the features.

List of insights obtained from the tests:

  • Dark Mode: Is not currently a priority feature, it can be deprioritized for now;
  • Homepage Optimization: Remove redundant options on "What are you looking for?" since they're all available in the menu;
  • Quiz Enhancement: Improve the quiz experience by indicating progress through "Question X of X" for clarity. Avoid asking too many questions to maintain user engagement;
  • Streamlining Adoption Search: The adoption section features an extensive page of filters to assist in finding the perfect pet, rendering the search bar redundant. Users can still choose the "See all" option to view all available pets without specific filters;
  • User Account and Settings: Consider removing features such as favorites, login, and profile since user accounts would only serve the purpose of maintaining a favorites list. Additionally, remove app settings as all customizable features have been eliminated;
  • Content Categorization: Combine general articles, training, and news into a single tab, introducing a search filter to compile similar information and reduce menu options;
  • Navigation Optimization: With the reduction of tabs to about five elements, explore placing the menu at the bottom of the screen for improved accessibility.

Test

Test Responses

The objective of these tests was to identify usability problems that users may encounter in the interface and receive feedback with the aim of improving it. Six participants were recruited and all performed the same tasks to allow comparison.
All participants successfully completed six tasks, except for task 2. In this particular task, which was finding the FAQ to clarify a question about adoption, only one participant managed to complete it. After completing the tasks, participants had the opportunity to freely explore the interface.
A brief exit questionnaire was conducted to gather their evaluations and suggestions. The prototype received an average rating of 8.5 out of 10.

Priority Revisions

Given the impossibility of implementing all suggested changes, prioritizing the findings became necessary. To achieve this, I employed the Nielsen Scale. After categorizing each suggestion, I chose to prioritize the four most problematic ones, as these were consistently mentioned by various users.

  • Since the button padding was small, making it difficult to navigate the interface, their padding was increased.
  • Since most users prefer to view all animals available for adoption first, the filter becomes optional. Thus, when clicking on “Adopt” in the menu, we are directed to the list of available animals.
  • Since around 85% of users did not find the FAQ, it was placed in the most suggested place, in “About Us”, followed by the contact form, in case the question was not clarified.
  • Considering that the adoption form is within each animal's profile, certain fields, such as the type of animal and its name, are pre-filled for user convenience.

Reflection & Next Steps

In order to prioritize the user experience, an iterative approach was adopted based on the User-Centered Design (UCD) process, where constant validation was sought among potential users. I believe this method was really effective in quickly identifying initial roadblocks to our users goals in the design and ensuring that the overall layout of content is effective and functional. If I were to push this project further, I would:

1. Re-test

Now that I have made revisions to my design, I would like to test and validate the changes made, observing if any further improvements need to be prioritized.

2. Design Handoff

With a finalized version of the design, I would present it to stakeholders and hand it off to developers to build the app.

3. Future Implementations

I believe there is room to add more value and delight to the experience by implementing additional features previously referenced in "Prioritize Features". Considering the implementation of a nationwide database, as suggested in interviews, to connect potential adopters with shelters across the country, allowing animals to move and increasing adoption opportunities. This project lays a promising starting point for future initiatives in the realm of animal welfare.
