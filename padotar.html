Padotar: Helping people find the perfect pet or related educational and informative content

This project is part of my master's thesis, so I will only refer to the content that actually corresponds to the mobile app and its research. However, if you are interested in reading the full text, you can do so here.

In this project I tried to combine my love for animals with a serious public health problem, as there is currently a significant increase in the number of abandoned animals, which results in a worrying situation that is placing a burden on animal protection and shelter associations. As we know, many animal shelters already have social media accounts and most of their advertising takes place there. But unlike these, where it's not possible to separate posts by topic, in this app we can find a variety of content categorized in such a way as to provide an intuitive user experience. Above all, the aim is to facilitate the adoption process, communication with the shelters and the dissemination not only of the activities carried out by them, but also the educational content that pet owners frequently search for.

Padotar is a fictitious shelter created purely for the project and the conception of the mobile app was restricted to the process of developing a digital product from a design perspective, so it was not implemented after the evaluation of the interactive hi-fi prototype.

Project Overview

Problem

No animal shelter in the research area offers a mobile app with adoption features, direct communication and properly organized informative and educational content.

Solution

Development of a mobile app so that any animal shelters can replicate it, as it was developed based on factors that will possibly contribute to increasing adoption levels.

Role

Research

Local Shelters Analysis

An analysis of the panorama of local animal shelters and the strategies implemented online was carried out. It was therefore found that, with the exception of one shelter, all others provide a web page or a profile on social media. However, none of them have their own mobile app. Out of 18 shelters:

  • 17 have a Facebook profile;
  • 13 have their own website;
  • 11 have an Instagram profile;
  • 2 of them have a Youtube channel;
  • But none of them have a mobile app.

Competitive Analysis

To get an idea of the strengths and weaknesses of possible competition, I carried out a brief survey of similar apps existing in Portugal. I say brief, since I only found one website matching the search criteria, and it's not even a mobile app.
We are talking about Petify, which is an interactive platform whose objective is to help abandoned animals find a family. This website essentially works like “lost and found”, where individuals or shelters can place advertisements for adoptions or lost animals.

Strengths

  • Increased visibility;
  • Community engagement;
  • Promotes civic responsibility;
  • Quick dissemination of information;
  • Educational opportunities.

Weaknesses

  • Accuracy of information;
  • Overwhelming volume;
  • Authenticity and privacy concerns;
  • Confirmation of adoptions;
  • Managing found pets.

Interviews

To carry out these interviews, people who work in institutions dedicated to the animal cause were selected in order to understand the needs of shelters, in order to inform the design of the product to be produced. In this case, the shelters are our stakeholders.
Two interviews were carried out during which participants had the opportunity to share problems and needs, contributing relevant information to the development of the project. In total, twelve questions were asked, divided into the general context of the shelter, the adoption process, the impact of new media and the implementation of alternative digital tools.
Subsequently, in a brief informal conversation with two possible users about the research topic of this project, some information emerged that was considered relevant not only for defining the functionalities to be implemented, but also for creating personas faithful to real experiences.

Findings

Summary of findings identified through literature review and interviews:

  • Size, behavior and personality are determining factors in adoption;
  • Give visibility to sponsorships, donations, members and volunteering;
  • It is important to raise awareness among future adopters;
  • Provide news about the shelter and the activities carried out by them;
  • Solidarity sales contribute to the financial sustainability of the shelter;
  • Maintain a coherent aesthetic and positive speech;
  • Clarify the most common doubts among pet owners.

Personas

These fictional characters represent the ideal users for the digital platform and, in this case, are based not only on the findings gathered from the interviews, but also on the experiences and needs of real people with whom I spoke briefly about the subject of this project. Three personas were created to match the three different types of users identified: people who work in shelters, people who have already adopted an animal and people who intend to adopt.

Define & Ideate

Prioritize Features

Due to the inherent temporal limitations within the project scope, I was confronted with the challenge of carefully selecting and prioritizing features for the app's design. The extensive list of proposed features needed to be streamlined in the face of limited time resources, so i decided to remove two features that were probably gonna take more time to create and yet didn't affect the main purpose of the project.

Features that move on to the next stage:

  • Search filter: allows the user to select the desired characteristics when searching for a pet;
  • Adopt: the aim is to show the user all the pets available for adoption and provide detailed information about each one. When selecting the option to adopt, the user is presented with a form, which once completed will be sent directly to the association;
  • Quiz: initial multiple-choice questionnaire for the user to understand whether they are ready to adopt. It will be an optional game that will appear as a highlight on the home page;
  • News: given the importance of informing users about what they are contributing to and who they are helping, it will be pertinent to provide news about the work and activities carried out by the shelter;
  • Articles: in order to reduce impulse adoptions, it is essential to include a feature that provides articles of an educational/informative nature, which support important decisions such as adopting an animal and inform about the respective care and inherent responsibilities;
  • Training: since most behaviors are a consequence of inadequate training or a lack of stimulation, it is important to provide content such as step by step training so that owners can easily follow and replicate them at home with their pets;
  • How to help: considering that many of these institutions survive thanks to the support received from their followers, information such as patronize, partners, volunteers and means of donation must have due visibility;
  • FAQ: so that there are no doubts about the adoption process, the most frequently asked questions will be available for any user to have access to.

Features that were excluded:

  • Pet hotel map: incorporating an interactive pet hotel map, designed to enhance the pet owners' experience by providing detailed, real-time information on available accommodations for their pets stay;
  • Solidary shop: since this is a common resource between shelters, it would be interesting to integrate a small solidarity store, in which all sales would go towards the financial sustainability of the association.

Branding

Branding

Now that i have refined the overall structure of the app, the question now was - what will it look like? Before working on the UI, I focused on creating Padotar's branding to effectively communicate the brand personality through the app.

Logo Design

Given the importance of visual identity in the implementation of digital products, I considered it pertinent to create a graphic brand. I tried above all to maintain a simple and minimalist aesthetic, in order to provide a legible and visually impactful image.
Therefore, the first step was to select the name of the fictitious shelter for which the application was developed. Since it is a shelter for animals looking for a family and the comfort of a home, the words “para” and “adotar” were combined. These become the word “padotar” which, coincidentally, results in a sound effect similar to their hurried pronunciation.
After selecting his name, a process of experimenting with the graphic brand began, using the digital tool Adobe Illustrator. As can be seen in the figure below, a brief study of colors was carried out, always associated with neutral tones that refer to pet fur. Above all, I tried to maintain a simple and minimalist aesthetic, in order to provide a legible and visually impactful image.

Once the brand was defined, I introduced the typography, giving rise to the logo.

Padotar's brand image is characterized by the symbol representing a dog and a cat inside a shelter, the animals most commonly found in these institutions, and by the logo, which obviously corresponds to the shelter's name.
Orange was chosen as the main color due to its contrast with other elements, and so the graphic brand will also convey the shelter's values, including joy, trust, strength and ambition. In order to maintain consistency with the interface design, this color will be used in other visual components.
For the logo, a simple sans-serif font, Futura, was chosen and a bold style was used to visually balance and highlight the shelter's name.

Brand Guide

UI Kit

In order to make the experience appealing to the user, I used a strong color full of personality, orange. As a complement, I selected a pastel shade, which represents a color analogous to orange, as it is found in its sequence within the color wheel. The fact that we use a pastel shade as opposed to the bright color itself, aims to convey serenity and comfort to the user. The shade of black is applied to the text and the RGB values are not at 100%, thus avoiding creating an excessively strong contrast, which would harm the vision of a user who stays in the app for a long time.
Regarding the selected fonts, the aim is simplicity, and in order to facilitate reading for people with reduced vision, dyslexia or cognitive and neurological difficulties, two sans serif fonts were defined, Futura and Avenir Next. The first one was previously used in the creation of the logo and, as such, to maintain visual coherence, it was applied to the titles. The second corresponds to all other written content, thus allowing the distinction between headings and paragraphs of text.

Prototype

Lo-fi Wireframe Sketches

Here's when I started to make decisions on how the content on Padotar's app would be organized based on the project goals I wanted to meet. I then developed a low-fidelity wireframe of the main pages, still with basic geometric shapes and few details, allowing focus on flow and features.

Paper Prototype

At this stage, it would be essential to deepen the interface in order to make the complete user flow visible and explore how to really make the process of searching for and adopting a pet as intuitive as possible. Here it is already possible to get an idea of how the interface will behave when performing more specific tasks.

Two people were asked to analyze and comment on the prototype in order to identify whether the flows were correct. That said, I decided to simplify and reduce some of the features.

List of insights obtained through these tests:

  • Dark mode isn't a relevant feature at the moment, I have to prioritize features;
  • On the home page, remove the “What are you looking for” options. It's redundant since they're all available in the menu;
  • In the quiz, inform the progress through “Question X of X”, because in percentage terms the user doesn't know how many questions there are. Do not ask too many questions to avoid making the activity boring;
  • When entering adoptions, we come across an entire page of filters to help you search for the ideal pet. The search bar here is meaningless as filters are provided for all important characteristics. If the user wants to view all the pets first, they will have the option to “See all”;
  • Remove favorites, login and profile, since the existence of a user would only make sense to maintain a favorites list;
  • In this case, the app settings are also useless, as all features that could be customizable have been removed;
  • Mix general articles, training and news, and put a search filter. This way, similar information is compiled in a single tab and the options listed in the menu are reduced;
  • With this reduction of tabs to about five elements, try to include the menu at the bottom of the screen.

Test

Test Responses

The objective of these tests was to identify usability problems that users may encounter in the interface and receive feedback with the aim of improving it. Six participants were recruited and all performed the same tasks to allow comparison.
Six tasks were assigned and all participants managed to complete them successfully, with the exception of task 2. In this particular task, even with difficulty, only one of the participants managed to complete it. This consisted of finding the FAQ to clarify a question about adoption.
Once the tests were finished, I gave them the opportunity to freely explore the interface and a brief exit questionnaire was carried out where participants were able to give their evaluation and suggestions. The prototype obtained an average rating of 8.5 out of 10.

Priority Revisions

Since it would not be possible to carry out all the suggested changes, it was necessary to prioritize the findings, so I used the Nielsen Scale to carry out this task. After classifying each suggestion, I decided to prioritize the 4 most problematic, which were also the most mentioned by different users.

  • Since the button padding was small, making it difficult to navigate the interface, their padding was increased.
  • Since most users prefer to view all animals available for adoption first, the filter becomes optional. Thus, when clicking on “Adopt” in the menu, we are directed to the list of available animals.
  • Since around 85% of users did not find the FAQ, it was placed in the most suggested place, in “About Us”, followed by the contact form, in case your question was not clarified.
  • Considering that the adoption form is within each animal's profile, data such as the type of animal and its name are now pre-filled.

Through extensive user feedback and iterative refinement, every pathway has been meticulously crafted to ensure effortless exploration and enhanced user satisfaction. Bellow, the updated version of the sitemap.

Reflection & Next Steps

In order to prioritize the user experience, an iterative approach was adopted based on the methodology of the UCD process, where constant validation was sought among potential users. I think this method was really effective in quickly identifying initial roadblocks to our user’s goals in the design and to make sure that the overall layout of content itself is effective and functional. If I were to push this project further, I would:

1. Re-test

Now that I made revisions to my design, I would like to test and validate the changes made and observe if any further improvements need to be prioritized.

2. Design Handoff

With a finalized version of the design, I would then present the final design to stakeholders and hand it off to developers to build the app.

3. Future Implementations

I think there’s room to add more value and delight to the experience by implementing other features previously referenced on "Prioritize Features". I also consider it relevant to mention that, in accordance with what was said in one of the interviews, it would be worth considering the implementation of a database at national level, which includes all the shelters and animals housed in them. In this way, and responding to the needs of potential adopters in any area of the country, allowing pets to move, giving them a greater chance of adoption. As such, the development of this project can be seen as a good starting point for future work within the animal cause.
